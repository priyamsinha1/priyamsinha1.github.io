Priyam Sinha

I am a media anthropologist and earned my PhD from the National University of Singapore in July 2024. Currently, I am a visiting research fellow at the Humboldt University zu Berlin. Curriculum vitae here.

My research interests include gender studies, disability studies, affect studies, production culture studies, critical new media industries studies, and the sociology of labour.

In my academic research, I have journeyed into journeys into the circulations of affect by proposing the framework of a “disability affect.” To explain the multifaceted constituents of disability representation, I examined the gendered labour practices, and the precarity and eventfulness of everyday experiences that influence cinema production and reception. Some of this work has been published in peer-reviewed journals like Media, Culture and Society, South Asian Diaspora, and the Journal of Indian and Asian Studies, across some editing books and handbooks and public writing forums like The Economic and Political Weekly, Asian Film Archive, Cafe Dissensus, among others. She is also a regular podcast host on the NewBooksNetwork.

Her published work can be found in the publications section.
